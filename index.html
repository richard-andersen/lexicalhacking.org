Lexical Hacking

lexical hacking (n.)

/ˈlɛksɪkəl ˈhækɪŋ/ (LEK-si-kul HAK-ing)

: the the good-faith, deliberate stabilization of an unanchored term by giving it a canonical definition — a durable semantic home

done early, it can lock meaning in place before drift, entropy, motte-and-bailey games, or bad-faith redefinitions can claim it

anchorjacking hallucinatory alignment
